Introduction

Homonyms are words that have the same pronounciation and spelling, but different meanings. For example, the words “free” and “free“.
Free and free are both spelled F–R–E–E, and are both pronounced the same, but have completely different meanings, making them homonyms. In this article, we will be focusing on free [of], meaning without, and free, meaning not costing anything.

Free socks are not free

[body]

This is because the first free in free free socks refers to the free meaning “without“, as in “is free of“. The second free then refers to the meaning of free as in “without cost“. The phrase “free free socks” then roughly translates to “without without cost socks“. Just like saying something is “not not blue“, the two “without”s cancel each other out, leaving you with the simple “[is] blue“, and, in our case, “[is cost] socks“.

Conclusion

We’ve now just proved that saying socks are “free free” is a void statement. The two “free”s cancel each other out, making the socks just that; socks. And just like any socks, free free socks aren’t free.
